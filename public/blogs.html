Why start a blog + Things I've been up to

Susan Sontag, an influential critic, writer, and filmmaker, once said, "To photograph is to confer importance." While she didn't specifically discuss blogging, we can extrapolate from her insight on photography to the act of blogging. To start a blog and write is to confer importance on your thoughts, experiences, and the projects you're working on. It's a way to document your journey, share insights, and engage with a community that has similar interests.

In my quest to document my projects, I see my blog as a vital tool not just for showcasing the outcomes, but also for articulating the process, challenges, and learnings along the way. Here's a succinct chronological list of my experiences:

  • Rhythmoji (Founder): Started with my roommate, a unique project where we convert Spotify listening data into a personalized Lego character.
  • Wavelength (Chief Technical Officer): Currently working at PLASMA (UC Davis Startup Accelerator), developing a music-based compatibility dating app focused on meaningful music data visualization.
  • Kogiito (Founder): An endeavor that has been a significant learning curve for my design and leadership skills, aiming to democratize brain science research to students worldwide.
  • Research Assistant at Integrated Attention Lab: Utilizing VR combined with eye-tracking technology to investigate naturalistic attention and memory, enhancing my research capabilities.

Each project has been a step in my journey, contributing to my skills, knowledge, and passion for merging human cognition with technology. Through my blog, I aim to share these experiences, the obstacles I've overcome, and the insights I've gained, hoping to inspire and engage others in this fascinating intersection of cognitive science and technology.
